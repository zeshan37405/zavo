Sunset Lamp Photography Ideas for Better Backgrounds

Learn how to use a sunset projection lamp for portraits, product photography and video backgrounds with practical distance and lighting tips.

Published July 22, 2026 · Zavo editorial guide

A sunset lamp can create an inexpensive colored background for portraits, product photos and short videos. The most useful results come from controlling three variables: the distance between the lamp and wall, the distance between the subject and wall, and the amount of other light in the room.

Start with the background before placing the subject

Aim the lamp at a plain wall and adjust the circle until the edge looks clean in the camera frame. Moving the lamp farther from the wall usually enlarges the projection, while moving it closer makes the circle smaller and more concentrated.

Keep the subject away from the wall

If a person or product sits directly against the wall, it may block the projection and create a hard shadow. Add some distance so the colored circle remains visible behind the subject. Even a small gap can improve separation.

Use the projection as background light, not face light

For portraits, use a separate window, desk lamp or soft light for the face. Let the sunset lamp color the wall. This approach keeps skin tones more natural while still producing a dramatic background.

Try an off-center composition

Place the bright center of the projection to one side of the subject. This can produce a more balanced image than placing the circle directly behind the head. Leave negative space where text may be added for a Pinterest Pin or social post.

Reduce unwanted room light

Turn off strong overhead lights and close curtains if daylight washes out the color. You do not need a completely dark room; you only need enough control for the projection to remain visible on camera.

Ideas for product photography

  • Place jewelry or beauty products in front of the colored circle.
  • Use a reflective surface to repeat the warm color below the item.
  • Project onto a paper backdrop for a cleaner studio look.
  • Photograph transparent or glossy objects at a slight angle to control reflections.

Ideas for portraits and video

  • Create a warm halo behind one shoulder.
  • Use purple or blue for a modern creator setup.
  • Move the lamp slowly between clips to create multiple backgrounds in one room.
  • Keep the camera exposure consistent when comparing colors.

Final photography checklist

  1. Frame the background first.
  2. Separate the subject from the wall.
  3. Add a neutral light for the subject.
  4. Check shadows and reflections.
  5. Capture several colors and angles before changing the setup.

The strongest image is usually not the brightest one. Aim for clear subject detail, a visible projection and enough contrast that the background supports the photo instead of overpowering it.
